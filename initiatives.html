Initiatives & Impact Models

Ideas for sustainable impact.

The models below guide how rightFOUNDATION initiatives are designed. They are presented as frameworks we believe in — verified programme results will be published as initiatives go live.

Development note: figures within models are illustrative concepts from internal planning. Publish only verified, approved data as confirmed results.
Model 01

Self-Sustaining Community Ecosystem

One connected ecosystem can link animal welfare, nutrition, community care, and affordable access. Revenue generated within the ecosystem — for example from a Goshala’s dairy operations — can sustain a community kitchen where anyone eats an affordable, wholesome meal, and the underprivileged receive the same meal at a symbolic price while contributing back with dignity.

Model 02

Collective SEWA

A shared-responsibility model: a company supplies tools or infrastructure, a training partner provides skills, a philanthropist supports meals or operating needs — and participants leave with employable capability. Even a modest contribution enables skilling and dignity when it is coordinated well.

Model 03

Capability-Led Development

We strengthen people rather than replace their effort. Every programme is designed around one principle: we do not serve by substitution; we serve by enablement. What remains after a programme ends is skill, dignity, and enduring capacity.
